📋 Tips & Tricks
  • Rebalance quarterly. If any position drifts more than ±5% from its target weight, trim the winner and top up the laggards. Forces you to buy low and sell high.
  • DCA beats timing. Set a fixed monthly amount (e.g. €500) and split it across your underweight positions. Removes emotion from the equation.
  • Never add to a loser without a thesis. "It's cheaper now" is not a reason. "Fundamentals intact and the dip is macro-driven" is.
  • Keep a cash buffer. 3–6 months of expenses before investing more. It prevents forced selling during crashes and lets you buy dips.
  • Tax-loss harvesting. In years with gains, sell losing positions to offset taxes. Rebuy a similar but not identical ETF to stay invested.
  • Review quarterly, not daily. Daily checks cause panic moves. Schedule a 30-min review every 3 months. Monthly at most.
  • Don't chase yesterday's winner. EUNL's +17% is beautiful, but don't double down. The best performer in any 3-year window is rarely the best in the next.
  • Separate "play money" from core. If you want to pick stocks, cap it at 10–15% of total. Keep 85%+ in boring, diversified ETFs.
  • Track cost basis. Know your entry price per position. Helps decide when to trim or add. Portfolio trackers like Parqet, Portfolio Performance (free) automate this.
  • Currency matters. NVD.DE is USD exposure in EUR wrapper. A weak dollar hurts you even if NVDA goes up. Mix EUR-hedged and unhedged positions.
